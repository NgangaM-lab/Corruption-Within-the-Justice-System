Introduction

Corruption is the abuse of power for personal gain, especially within institutions responsible for fairness and law enforcement. This project explores whether corruption within justice systems contributes to rising crime, using global data and a Ukrainian case study.

Main Causes

  • Weak accountability structures
  • Excessive official discretion
  • Underfunded justice institutions
Estimated impact of key corruption drivers

Consequences

  • Restricted access to justice
  • Increased inequality before the law
  • Public mistrust in institutions
  • Rise of mob justice and crime

Global vs National Perspectives

Global: Countries with strong institutions experience less corruption and higher public satisfaction.

Ukraine Case: Major protests in 2020 and 2025 highlighted deep public mistrust and demand for anti-corruption reforms.

Conclusion: Corruption consistently weakens justice systems and contributes to crime through mistrust and inequality.
